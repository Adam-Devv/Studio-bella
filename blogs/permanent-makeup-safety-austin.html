Why Sterility is Crucial in Permanent Makeup

Permanent makeup (PMU), also known as cosmetic tattooing or micropigmentation, involves depositing colored pigment into the upper layer of the dermis. Because this process involves breaking the skin, adhering to strict sterilization protocols is absolutely essential to prevent the risk of infection and ensure safe, beautiful results. Compromised safety can lead to health complications and unsatisfactory outcomes – something we meticulously guard against.

Studio Bella's Multi-Layered Approach to Your Safety

Your well-being is our top priority. We've implemented comprehensive safety measures that meet and often exceed industry regulations. Here’s how we ensure a safe and sterile experience for every client visiting our Austin studio:

Hospital-Grade Sterilization Protocols

We utilize hospital-grade sterilization methods, including autoclaves, for all reusable equipment. This ensures that any instruments not designed for single-use are thoroughly sterilized before every procedure, eliminating potential contaminants.

Single-Use Needles and Supplies are Standard

For any components that directly contact your skin during the pigmentation process, single-use is our standard. This includes brand new, sterile, individually packaged needles and cartridges for every single client, every single time. These items are safely disposed of immediately after your procedure, removing any possibility of cross-contamination.

Premium, Safe Pigments

The quality of the pigments used is vital not only for achieving gorgeous, lasting color but also for safety. We use high-quality pigments from reputable manufacturers known for their safety standards and rigorous testing. We understand the concerns about pigments fading into undesirable colors (as highlighted in online discussions) and prioritize stable, safe pigment choices suitable for your skin type and desired look.
